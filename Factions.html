What is a Mixed Fraction?

A mixed fraction has a whole number and a fraction.

It shows more than one whole, plus an extra part.

🍕 Example 1: 1 12 = 1 whole and 1 half
🍰 Example 2: 2 34 = 2 wholes and 3 quarters
🍫 Example 3: 3 13 = 3 wholes and 1 third
More examples
  • 425 = 4 wholes and 2 fifths
  • 538 = 5 wholes and 3 eighths
  • 6710 = 6 wholes and 7 tenths

A mixed fraction is easy to read because it shows the whole number first.

What is an Improper Fraction?

An improper fraction has a numerator bigger than the denominator.

It represents a value greater than 1 whole.

5 2

5 is bigger than 2

7 4

7 is bigger than 4

9 3

9 is bigger than 3

More examples: 115 136 178 2110

Convert Mixed → Improper

Multiply the whole number by the denominator, then add the numerator.

Rule:
(whole × denominator) + numerator → put the answer over the same denominator
212 → (2 × 2) + 1 = 5 → 52
314 → (3 × 4) + 1 = 13 → 134
423 → (4 × 3) + 2 = 14 → 143
534 → (5 × 4) + 3 = 23 → 234
615 → (6 × 5) + 1 = 31 → 315
178 → (1 × 8) + 7 = 15 → 158

Convert Improper → Mixed

Divide the numerator by the denominator.

The quotient is the whole number. The remainder becomes the numerator.

72 → 7 ÷ 2 = 3 remainder 1 → 312
94 → 9 ÷ 4 = 2 remainder 1 → 214
113 → 11 ÷ 3 = 3 remainder 2 → 323
135 → 13 ÷ 5 = 2 remainder 3 → 235
176 → 17 ÷ 6 = 2 remainder 5 → 256
227 → 22 ÷ 7 = 3 remainder 1 → 317

Different Denominators

Adding fractions with different denominators

First find a common denominator, then add the numerators.

Step-by-step method
Step 1: Look at the denominators.
Step 2: Find a common denominator.
Step 3: Convert both fractions so they have the same denominator.
Step 4: Add the numerators.
Step 5: Keep the denominator the same and simplify if needed.
Examples
Example 1:
12 + 14

Common denominator = 4
12 = 24
24 + 14 = 34
Example 2:
23 + 16

Common denominator = 6
23 = 46
46 + 16 = 56

Different Denominators

Subtracting fractions with different denominators

First find a common denominator, then subtract the numerators.

Step-by-step method
Step 1: Look at the denominators.
Step 2: Find a common denominator.
Step 3: Convert both fractions so they have the same denominator.
Step 4: Subtract the numerators.
Step 5: Keep the denominator the same and simplify if needed.
Examples
Example 1:
34 − 12

Common denominator = 4
12 = 24
34 − 24 = 14
Example 2:
56 − 13

Common denominator = 6
13 = 26
56 − 26 = 36 = 12

Subtracting with Different Denominators
Finding Common Denominators

What if the denominators are NOT multiples?

We find the smallest number both denominators can turn into.

Step-by-step method
Step 1: List multiples.
Example: 3 → 3, 6, 9, 12, 15
5 → 5, 10, 15
Step 2: Find the first number they share.
15 is the common denominator.
Step 3: Convert both fractions so they have the same denominator.
Important: Whatever you multiply the denominator by, you must multiply the numerator by the same number.

Non-Multiple Denominators
Key Steps

Always follow these steps.

1. Convert mixed fractions to improper fractions if needed.
2. Make sure the denominators are the same.
3. Add or subtract the numerators.
4. Keep the denominator the same.
5. Simplify the answer.
6. Change back to a mixed number if needed.
